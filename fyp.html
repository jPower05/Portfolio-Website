Fourth Year Project

Redirection

Redirection is a fast paced 3D multiplayer co-op puzzle game for the PC developed on the Unity 3D game engine and written in C#

A team of two players on the network must cooperate and use teamwork in order to solve puzzles and escape. They escape by redirecting the room’s power source using mirrors and similar objects. Directing the power source into the correct positions overrides the door locking mechanisms which open the doors allowing the beings to progress eventually to freedom. They have a limited amount of light energy (representing time) to solve each puzzle. Puzzles are progressive and follow a difficulty curve where difficulty of puzzles increases as player skill grows.

Networking is accomplished using Unity Networking (UNet) and games are hosted on Unity’s cloud servers. For development Scrum is used however some allowances are made due to there being only a single developer. Redirection has a supporting narrative. You play as an ‘Irok’ which is a noncorporeal light form. You find yourself trapped on a ship called the ‘Cydonia’ floating around in space. A power cut has given you a chance to escape from your holdings. You must work together to gain freedom.
